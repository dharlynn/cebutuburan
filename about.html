HISTORY OF TUBURAN

The Local Government Unit of the Municipality of Tuburan had its land use plan in the year 1983 or 17 years ago. The growth of the Municipality as the planning area since its latest planning exercise in terms of socio political and socio-economic development is concrete and observable that required a redirection of its development and revision of its objectives and target as well as land use.

Tuburan is the birthplace of General Arcadio Maxilom, the highest revolutionary leader in Cebu, and is situated in the northwestern part of the province. It is 96 kilometer from the City of Cebu via the Toledo road and 104 kilometer by way of Lugo, Borbon.

The town got its name because of the presence of several spring known as Mantawihan, Guiwanon, Tubod, Saksak and Molobolo. All of these sources of water serve as bathing resort of picnickers.

Before one of its barrios, Tabuelan, was separated and made a municipality, on recommendation of the Governor Sergio Osmeña, Jr. of Cebu, Tuburan was the largest town in the whole province of Cebu both in area and in population. Presently, it is classified as a second class municipality with an income of P79,000,000.00 as of calendar year 2011.

In September, 1952, amidst great rejoicings, Tuburan celebrate the first centennial anniversary of its founding as a municipality in the year 1852. It was recalled that the first head to occupy the dignified position as “Capitan” was Mariano Montebon.

During the revolution against Spain, almost all able-bodied men in Tuburan took active part in the arms uprising against the tyrannical rule in Spain. One memorable event during the war was the complete annihilation of one company of Spain soldiers by the revolutionist who were only armed with bolos, daggers and spears. Their leader was Bonifacio Arcenas assisted by Ramon Allego who distinguished himself in the encounter by using his second sword with dexterity. He did much of the killing.

When, as a result of the cession of the Philippine to the United States by Spain, the America occupied Cebu, the patriots of the province again rose in revolt. Their new leader was General Arcadio Maxilom who led his men against the America forces. He fought several bloody encounters but his ill-equipped troops were finally overcome and forced to surrender.

Some months after the fall if Bataan in the last war, two young men of Tuburan, Capitan Columbus Parilla and Sarhento Simon Maxilom. organized a Guerilla force. The unit operated effectively in the hills. It was finally attached to the 86th and 88th regiments of the Cebu area command of the Philippines Resistance against the Japanese invaders.

Nature has endowed Tuburan with limited area of rich agricultural lands best suited for growing corn, rice, sugarcane. Coconuts and other crops. Besides farming, which is the most important occupation of the people, those who live along the coasts engage in fishing about two decades ago. The daily catch of fish in big quantities were sent by trucks to the neighboring towns and to the City of Cebu, but recent years showed the decreasing fish catch due to coastal resource degradation.

Population

The population of the municipality of Tuburan has an annual growth rate of 2.61% based on the Acquired population Growth Rate 2007-2010 Population Census conducted by the National Census and Statistical Office (NCSO). As of year 2010 Population Census, The urban population constitutes 16.25% of the total municipal population. The rural population on the one hand occupies 83.75% as of the same year.

In 2007, the planning area had a population of 53,663. In 2010, it increased to 58,914, showing an increase of 5,251 population or 9.78 % in a span of 5 years. By 2017, the municipal population is projected to reach 76,937 and 93,767 in 2022. Definitely, the population increase, as projected from 2013-2022, needs exactly the same socio-economic services and facilities which the government should look forward to provide it’s people.

Basing on the 2010 Population Census, the Poblacion as the Urban Area has the biggest population with 9,572 inhabitants. It is followed by barangays Putat, Cogon, Colonia, Carmelo and Daan-Lungsod. So far, municipal records would show that these barangays outside the urban areas are for Nodal Point development.

Furthermore, there is an uneven distribution of population in town. This is so because people settle where they have something for livelihood, shelter, enjoyment and water.

Land - Climate

Land

The Tuburan municipality is 29,316 hectares of land traversed by seven (7) river systems namely; the Langoyon River, Putat River, Tunlob River, Adela River, Apalan River, Bakyawan-Caridad River and Bagasawe River. Each river system is the common drainage of land areas the main outlet of which is the river that leads to the sea, these are called watershed areas. Tuburan then is largely composed of six (6) watershed areas namely the Langoyon River Watershed, the Putat River Watershed, the Bakyawan River Watershed, the Tubon River Watershed, the Adela River Watershed and the Bagasawe River Watershed. On the other hand, approximately 400 hectares watershed area is without drainage particularly Fortaliza-Colonia watershed.

Climate

The Municipality of Tuburan falls under the third type of climate in the Philippines, characterized by not very pronounced maximum period of rainfall and with a short dry season from March to June. The highest recorded temperature usually happens in the month of May with 30.9 degrees celcius (˚C ) while the lowest recorded temperature occurs in the month of January with 24.6 degrees celcius (˚C ).

Tuburan District Hospital

Since 1963, the Municipality had acquired an Emergency Hospital-The Tuburan Hospital categorically classified as a level 1 Hospital in the province. Headed by an energetic development-oriented Doctor the hospital had fifty (50) personnels, fourteen (14) of which are contractual employees. The Hospital had four Physicians (2 are contractuals); 5 Nursing Attendants; 1 Medical Technologist; Nutrionist/Dietician; Pharmacist; Radiologic Technologist; Midwife; Dentist; Dental Aide; Administrative Officer/Accountant; Disbursing Officer; Storekeeper; Cook and Maintenance Man. There are 7 Utility Workers; 2 Laundry workers; 3 Clerks; 2 Drivers and 4 Security Guards. Tuburan District Hospital had 25 bed capacity and 2 ambulances for the mobility of patients. More continuing programs regarding health cares, awareness and monitoring of health conditions are in place for implementation.


Rural Health Units

The Municipality of Tuburan had two (2) Rural Health Units-The RHU I Office located in the Poblacion and the RHU II in Sangi, Brgy. Mangga. The two units are headed by a responsive Physician in the office. RHU I is manned by a resident Physician, seven (7) Midwives and a Nurse, serving the urban area. RHU II had six (6) Midwives and two Nurses serving the rural areas in mountain barangays. The Barangay Health Workers formed themselves into an Association, the BHWA of Tuburan comprising of 271 active members in operation, working for the healthy environment in the municipality.

Vision

A peaceful and progressive Tuburan, with God-fearing, and responsible citizenry, dwelling in an ecologically balanced environment; and a dependable, committed, and sincere Leadership; with honest conviction to promote transparency, accountability, and participatory decision making, geared to become a tourist destination and economic hub of the Province of Cebu.

Mission

1. Promote responsible, healthy, values laden, educated law-abiding, and empowered citizenry
2. Proper management, protection, preservation, and rehabilitation of all environment and natural resources
3. Provision of favorable, wholesome investment climate to promote job opportunities
4. Promotion of high standard of education in schools;
5. Institute for a clean progressive, and peaceful Tuburan
6. Preservation of heritage and culture.
