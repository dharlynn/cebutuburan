Nature Tourism

Marmol Cliffs

The Marmol Cliffs are essentially two rocks that form a passageway for a river running through it. The name of the place is based on the stone walls that are smooth and shiny and have similarities to the marble stone. The word “marmol” is the Cebuano term used for marble. There are instances late in the afternoon when the sun would reflect on a particular part of the wall, which shows how smoothness of the face of the rock. More often than not the river running between the hard rock walls is shallow enough for people to wade through. But, there are also instances when the water level of the river would rise up to twenty feet, especially during heavy rainfall. During these times, the calm, shallow river would rage and endanger the lives of anyone foolhardy enough to cross through it. The walls of the rock form a 90-degree angle providing visitors a gorge-like view. The crevices of the wall have some plant life growing in them while swallows can be seen darting around the place. You can get a ride on a motorcycle for hire to take you to the place. If you are bringing your own vehicle, it is advisable to leave it in the town since it will be necessary to go through the river to get to the cliff. It is better to hire a motorcycle in going to the cliff since the drivers know how to go through the river using its shallow parts.

Adela River

Adela River is one of the grandest rivers in Tuburan, Cebu. It is approximately 3 kilometers stretch going through lined nipa palm trees and passes by three of the many natural springs of Tuburan. In light of the river, they made a bridge that linked Daan Lungsod and Brgy. 2. The river is connected from San Juan, and Mantawihan waterway, the water of the river flows straightforwardly to the sea. Several individuals jump at the bridge and swim directly to the the river particularly youngsters during red-tide. The Local Government Unit of Tuburan is on its way for developing the river.

Molobolo Resort

Molobolo resort is one of the popular places to be refreshed and relaxed in Tuburan Municipality, Cebu Province, Philippines. Tuburan means ‘source of water’ or spring in Cebuano or Bisayan language. The springs of this place are the greatest endowment to the inhabitants of this area. The spring water is being channeled into two big pools. The bigger pool is about 7 feet deep and is intended for adults and good swimmers. The other and smaller pool is for kids who are mostly the frequent and the biggest in number among users. The canopy of the trees that blankets the pools keep away the sunlight, thus, making the spring water much cooler and rejuvenating to be enjoyed with. The Molobolo Spring management only charges very cheap entrance fees to users (5pesos for adults and 2pesos for kids). The parking fee for 4-wheelers is Php100 and 25 for 2-wheelers (motorbikes). The bigger cottages can be rented for Php300 while the smaller type is Php150.

Dao Tree

The Heritage Tree of Jagbuaya in Tuburan is around two centuries old and has seen history unfold. The tree is a Dao tree, which is a forest tree with huge roots to support the weight of its thick canopy. It is a part of the Dracontomelon Dao and is also known as the Argus pheasant tree or the New Guinea walnut tree. The species is considered threatened and is a part of the “Red List” of the International Conservation Union. These trees typically grow in swamps and lowland areas in southern Asia. Aside from the Philippines, the tree can also be found in India and southern China. The diameter of the Heritage Tree of Jagbuaya in Tuburan is around seven meters. Since the tree is not located along the national highway, it is necessary to ask residents for directions to get to it. The tree may not look impressive when observed from afar, but once visitors would be amazed at its immense size once they would stand beside it. The Heritage Tree of Jagbuaya in Tuburan is one tree that survived many generations and continues to stand up to this date.

Sea and Beach Tourism

Apalan Marine Sanctuary

Apalan Marine Sanctuary was established sometime in 2003 by the Municipality of Tuburan, Cebu with a size of 53 hectares. Presently, these are considered as ideal diving and snorkeling sites with healthy reef and abundant growth of soft and hard corals housing fishes, and other colorful aquarium species.

Lantawan Tuburan Resort

Lantawan Tuburan Resort is located in Panas Daan Lungsod, the place is scenic. It is owned by former Mayor of the municipality. It has a vast ground area in front of the resort covered with mango trees with thick lush green grasses underneath.

Cultural, Historical Tourism and Festival

San Antonio de Padua Church

San Antonio de Padua Church is is a pilgrim destination for those devoted to San Antonio de Padua, the patron of good business, sailors, expectant mothers, the poor and lost The structure is a semi-Romanesque design and was constructed more than 100 years ago. Located at the middle town of the Municipality.

Tuburan Town Musem

The Arcadio Maxilom Memorial Library in the Tuburan Museum is named after his death. It was opened last April 12, 2008 by Hon. housed in what was originally the ancestral house of Don Diego Tabotabo, one of the earliest settlers and administrators of Essentially, the museum is a tribute to the history and culture of the town from pre-history to the present

Tubod Festival

The Tubod Festival is held every 13th day of the month of June. Aside from showing their gratitude for the natural blessings bestowed on the town, the annual festival is also celebrated in honor of the patron saint of the town, St. Anthony de Padua. The festival is one of the most-anticipated events in the town among Tuboranons.

Budbud

It is usually a rice cake snack or dessert wrapped in banana leaves, while pilit (“pee-lit”), which means “to stick. It’s the simplest item in the Filipino (sweet) rice-cake universe, using just coconut milk, sugar, and salt as flavoring agents.

Bibingka

It is a type of rice cake from the Philippines and in Christian communities in Indonesia. It is usually eaten for breakfast, especially during the Christmas season. It is traditionally cooked in clay pots lined with leaves.
